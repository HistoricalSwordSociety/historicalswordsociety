Treatise Library

The Historical Sword Society Treatise Library provides curated primary sources, translations, and scholarly materials related to historical European martial arts (HEMA). Our focus includes rapier systems, Verdadera Destreza, and broader historical fencing traditions.

These documents are intended for academic study, technical interpretation, and practical application in modern training environments. Wherever possible, original-language sources are preserved alongside translations.

Primary Sources & Core Treatises

  • Compendio de los Fundamentos de la Verdadera Destreza
    Foundational Spanish Destreza text outlining core principles and geometric framework.
  • Nobleza de la Espada — Book Three (English Translation)
    Advanced theoretical and applied concepts from one of the most important Destreza works.
  • Tamariz — Cartilla y Luz en la Verdadera Destreza
    Instructional material focused on pedagogy and structured learning of Destreza.

Supplementary Research & Interpretation

  • Destreza — Epic Study & Interpretation
    Modern interpretive analysis bridging historical theory with contemporary practice.
  • Paris Military Fencing Tournament — Official Rule Set
    Historical competitive framework reflecting later military fencing evolution.

Usage & Contributions

All documents are provided for educational and research purposes. Users are encouraged to cross-reference sources, compare translations, and engage critically with the material.

If you encounter a broken link or would like to contribute a public-domain treatise, translation, or scholarly work, please contact the Historical Sword Society.
