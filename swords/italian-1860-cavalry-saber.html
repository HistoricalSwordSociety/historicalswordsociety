Italian 1860 Cavalry Saber

Type / Pattern: Italian 1860 Cavalry Saber
Approx. Date: Mid-19th century, circa 1860
Origin: Italy – cavalry pattern
Current Status: Private collection (Historical Sword Society)

Abstract: The Italian 1860 Cavalry Saber represents a mid-nineteenth-century European cavalry weapon shaped by the dominant influence of French military sword design. Featuring a curved blade optimized for cutting from horseback, it reflects the continued emphasis on slashing attacks in mounted combat during this period. Its form illustrates the broader adoption and adaptation of continental saber patterns across European armies prior to the later shift toward thrust-oriented military doctrine.
